The Pawn

The pawn is the smallest and most numerous chess piece. It moves forward one square at a time but captures diagonally. When a pawn reaches the opposite end of the board, it can be promoted to a more powerful piece.

The Rook

The rook moves in straight lines across ranks or files. It plays a vital role in both defense and attack, especially in the endgame.

The Knight

The Bishop

The bishop moves diagonally, staying on squares of one color. It is especially powerful in open positions.

The Queen

The queen is the most powerful piece, combining the movement of the rook and bishop. It dominates the board with its versatility.

The King

The king is the most important piece. It moves one square in any direction. The game ends when the king is checkmated.

Famous Chess Players

Garry Kasparov: Dominated chess from 1985 to 2000, known for his aggressive playing style and strategic brilliance.

Magnus Carlsen: The current World Chess Champion, celebrated for his endgame mastery and intuitive play.

Bobby Fischer: An American chess legend who became World Champion in 1972. Renowned for his genius and innovation.
